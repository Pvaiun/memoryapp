Calming the calendar

The month grid currently claims about 60% of the screen — 42 boxed tiles, rainbow dots, and the actual schedule squeezed into two rows at the bottom. These three static mock-ups keep Memory's existing dark palette, accent, and type, and each one reduces the noise a different way.

Same layout, most of the weight removed. The tiles disappear: day numbers sit directly on the background, so an empty day is just a quiet number and a scheduled day visibly carries something. Days outside July vanish instead of ghosting.

  • 1The grid drops from ~430px to ~270px tall by giving up the square aspect ratio. Nothing is lost — the tiles were mostly padding — and the agenda gains two to three visible rows.
  • 2Today and selected become circles, not tiles. A thin accent ring marks today; a filled accent circle marks the selection. Both read instantly because they're the only enclosed shapes left in the grid.
  • 3Agenda rows get one metadata line, ever. Flavour moves into a theme-tinted glyph chip on the left, so the text line only carries what varies: cadence, range, theme. Titles stop wrapping under the noise.

Cheapest to ship: this is mostly CSS on .cal-cell / .cal-grid in src/app/styles.css, hiding .other cells, and a slimmer row renderer in the agenda section of CalendarView.tsx.

Flip the hierarchy: the schedule is the page, the grid is navigation. The month collapses into a single week strip; tapping the month name (▼) expands the full grid only when you need to jump far. Everything below is a continuously scrolling agenda for the days ahead, grouped under day headers.

  • 1"What's coming?" needs zero taps. Today, tomorrow, and the weekend are all on screen at once — seven-plus entries visible instead of two. This matches how the tab is actually used day to day.
  • 2One accent-colored tick per day replaces the rainbow. Its strength scales with how much is scheduled, so the strip previews density without color noise.
  • 3Swiping the strip moves a week at a time; the expanded month (rendered as Direction 3's quiet grid) handles long jumps, then collapses again.

Bigger lift: CalendarView.tsx needs a collapsed/expanded state and a multi-day query window (the api.calendar(from, to) range already supports it), plus the grouped agenda renderer.

Keep the full month, retire the rainbow. Each day gets at most one mark: a short accent-colored underline whose opacity steps with how much is scheduled. Density now reads pre-attentively — the busy back half of July is visible from arm's length — while the grid stays a single hue.

  • 1One encoding, one color. The underline answers the only question a month view can answer at this size: "how full is that day?" Theme colors move to the agenda, where there's room for the tinted glyph chips to actually be labeled.
  • 2A three-step legend makes the encoding honest — something the current dots never had.
  • 3Pairs naturally with Direction 2: this grid is what the expanded month should look like when the week strip unfolds.

Small change: CalendarView.tsx already has per-day counts in byDay; the dots span becomes one bar with an opacity class, plus the Direction 1 CSS cleanup.

Recommendation

Ship 1 now, grow into 2 + 3

Direction 1 is an afternoon of work — mostly CSS — and removes the bulk of the overwhelm: tile noise gone, grid 40% shorter, agenda readable. Do it regardless of what comes next.

The structural fix is Direction 2. A calendar tab on a phone is opened to answer "what's coming?", and the week-strip-plus-agenda answers it with zero taps. When the month needs to expand for long jumps, render it as Direction 3's quiet grid — one accent hue, density underlines, legend — so the two directions compose into a single coherent page.
